built an interactive HR Attrition Dashboard using Excel and Power Query to analyze employee turnover and uncover the key drivers behind attrition.
🔹 Data Preparation
•	Removed irrelevant fields ( EmployeeCount, Over18, StandardHours).
•	Created a numeric AttritionFlag (Yes = 1, No = 0) for KPI calculations.
•	Standardized categorical values (e.g., BusinessTravel).
•	Grouped continuous variables such as Age and Monthly Income into ranges (Age Groups, Salary Slabs) to reveal clearer patterns.
•	Leveraged Power Query for data cleaning, transformation.
🔹 Key Insights
•	Sales Department shows the highest attrition rate (20.6%), followed by HR (19%).
•	Younger employees (18–25) have the highest attrition rate (34.8%).
•	Single employees represent the majority of attrition cases (51%).
•	Frequent business travelers are more likely to leave compared to non travelers.
•	Lower salary slabs (<5k) are strongly correlated with higher attrition.
•	Employees with (<5) years at the company account for the largest share of attrition.
